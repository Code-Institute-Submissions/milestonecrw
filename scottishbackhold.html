SCOTTISH BACKHOLD WRESTLING

Scotlands traditional martial art

Combat Ready are proud to be the first and only club in Edinburgh and the surrounding area to offer this indigenous martial art to the public. Our aim is to help The Scottish Wrestling Bond (Backholds governing body) bring this wonderful martial art back from the brink of extinction. If you’d like to part of this then contact us for further info on classes.

Scottish Backhold Wrestling History

The first Scottish representations of wrestling appear in early carved stones, one from each of the Celtic kingdoms, which make up modern Scotland. In the period shortly after the Norse invasions there are a number of references to wrestling, which was an essential part of the training of a warrior in order to fully develop the skills vital for survival in hand to hand combat. Some researchers suggest that the commencing position of backhold owes its origin to the need to try and trap an opponent’s sword arm before throwing him to the ground. Scottish Backhold is a style of wrestling that originated in Scotland. The wrestlers grip each other around the waist at the back, with the right hand under the opponents left arm and the chin resting on the opposite right shoulder. Once the referee is sure both wrestlers have a firm grip he shouts HOLD and the bout begins. Should either wrestler break his hold or touch the ground with any part of his body except his feet he loses. There is no ground work involved in Scottish Backhold and bouts are usually best of five falls.
